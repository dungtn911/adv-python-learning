The ongoing pandemic of coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) spread to
the United Kingdom in late January 2020.[3] Transmission within the UK
was confirmed in late February,[4] and the number of cases rose swiftly
in March.[5] As of 20 April, there have been 124,743 confirmed cases in
the UK[nb 3][2] and 17,337 people with confirmed infections have
died,[nb 1][2] a rate of about 200 deaths per million population.[11]
More than 90% of those dying have underlying illnesses[12] and are over
60 years old.[13] There are large regional variations in the pandemic's
severity. The capital, London, has the highest number and highest rate
of infections.[14] England and Wales are the countries with the highest
recorded death rate per capita, while Northern Ireland has the lowest
recorded death rate.[15] Freedom of movement and assembly are restricted
for the whole population, and most public venues are closed.

The first confirmed case of the pandemic of coronavirus disease 2019
(COVID-19) in the United Arab Emirates was announced on 29 January 2020.
It was the first country in the Middle East to report a confirmed
case.[2] The first patient, a 73-year-old Chinese woman, was released on
9 February after recovering. The first two deaths were confirmed on
March 20. On 22 March, Dubai started an 11-day sterilisation campaign as
an effort to contain the coronavirus. Night curfew was imposed 4 days
later while the country began disinfection. School closure was first
announced on March 8 for 4 weeks. 3 weeks later, it was announced that
school will be closed until the end of the academic year.
